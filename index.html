Homebrew Kombucha: An Easy How To

by Traci Emerson

A cheaper way to have that yummy, fizzy, gut-helping drink on hand.

Yields 6(ish) Quarts

Ingredients:

  • 2 Chicken Breasts, cubed
  • 1 - 16 oz. Pkg Rice Noodles
  • 3 TBSP Coconut Oil
  • 1 Clove Garlic
  • 3 Eggs
  • 2 TBSP Fish Sauce
  • 2 TBSP Rice Vinegar
  • 1 Green Onion
  • 2 TBSP Sugar(or alternative sweetener, to taste)
  • 2 TBSP Peanut Butter
  • 1 TSP Paprika
  • 1/4 TSP Cayenne Pepper (optional, to taste)
  • Bean Sprouts (optional)
  • Peanuts, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix fish sauce, rice vinegar, peanut butter, sugar (or sweetener), paprika, and cayenne (if you choose to include it), and heat over low heat in a saucepan until thoroughly mixed together. Set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, soak dry rice noodles in hot (preferably boiling) water to soften.
  3. Heat coconut oil in a medium size pan (hint: a pot works too and makes less of a mess), over medium heat. Once the oil is melted, heat the chicken until fully cooked.
  4. Drain the softened noodles and add to the cooked chicken. Mix until noodles begin to stick to the pan.
  5. Turn down heat to low, and pour fish sauce mixture over chicken and noodles and mix until all the noodles are evenly colored.
  6. Serve and top with green onions, bean sprouts, and/or chopped peanuts.
